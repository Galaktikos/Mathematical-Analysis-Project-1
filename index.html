Logarithmic Equation

To start solving a logarithmic equation, you should first combine all logarithmic functions on the same side into one equation. The same concept applies with subtraction, except the numbers are divided instead of multiplied.
You then must make the two combined values into one by multiplying or dividing them together.
Then make both sides the exponent of the logarithm's base value. If there is no base shown on the logarithm then it is 10.
The logarithm exponent can be simplified to only the value inside it and the other side can be solved by finding 10 to the power of 3.
The 1000 on the right side of the equation can then be divided by the 10 on the left side of the equation.
The x to the second power can be solved by taking the square root of both sides, leaving a plus or minus 10 as the answer.
Plugging both the positive and negative solutions back in to the equation shows that only the positive solution still works since logarithms cannot contain a negative.
